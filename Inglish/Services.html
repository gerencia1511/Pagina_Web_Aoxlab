Description

At Aoxlab, microbiological analysis represents a comprehensive set of tests designed to detect, identify, quantify and characterize microorganisms in a wide variety of matrices, including food, water, raw materials, finished products, contact surfaces, production environments and biological materials. Our microbiological services are oriented towards guaranteeing safety, regulatory compliance and microbiological quality in the agri-food, cosmetic, pharmaceutical, environmental, medicinal cannabis and other sectors.

Importance for Industry

Microbiological analysis is fundamental to ensuring the quality, safety and integrity of products throughout the production chain. In an increasingly regulated and demanding environment, these services allow companies to prevent health risks, comply with national and international regulations, and protect consumer health.

For industry, having reliable microbiological control is key to:

  • Prevent cross-contamination and outbreaks associated with pathogenic microorganisms such as Salmonella, Listeria monocytogenes or E. coli STEC.
  • Ensure good manufacturing and laboratory practices (GMP, GLP) through surveillance of environments, surfaces and personnel.
  • Comply with export regulatory requirements, certification and labeling.
  • Make informed decisions about shelf life, preservation and product formulation.

By integrating these services into their quality strategy, industries strengthen their competitiveness, minimize legal risks and reinforce market and consumer confidence.

Importance for Industry

Mycotoxins are toxic compounds produced by certain fungi that can contaminate raw materials and finished products, especially in food, supplements, feed, cannabis products and cosmetics with natural ingredients. Their analysis is critical for consumer safety and product commercial viability.

Key reasons why they are important:

Public health and consumer protection

Mycotoxins, such as aflatoxins and ochratoxins, are associated with serious effects like cancer, liver damage, immunosuppression and kidney toxicity. Their detection prevents consumer exposure to these risks.

Regulatory compliance and exports

Organizations like Codex Alimentarius, FDA (USA), EFSA (European Union) and even INVIMA in Colombia establish maximum permitted limits for mycotoxins. Complying with these limits is essential to avoid sanctions, export rejections and market loss.

Prevention of economic losses

Mycotoxin contamination can lead to rejection of entire batches, merchandise destruction, contract loss or lawsuits. Analyzing them on time reduces these costs.

Quality control and business reputation

Companies that implement mycotoxin analytical controls reinforce their commitment to quality and safety, improving their credibility with clients, certifiers and regulatory entities.

Certification requirements

Systems like GMP, HACCP, BRC, FSSC 22000 and other food safety schemes require mycotoxin monitoring as part of hazard analysis.

Shelf Life and Stability Studies

At Aoxlab we conduct tailor-made shelf life and stability studies, fundamental to support the safety, quality, efficacy and regulatory compliance of products.


Food, cosmetics, supplements, phytotherapeutics and medicinal cannabis. These studies allow us to scientifically define the time during which a product retains its properties under specific conditions, being a key requirement for sanitary registrations, national commercialization and export. We have the infrastructure, technical knowledge and experience to develop: Studies under normal conditions (real time) Accelerated studies (Arrhenius model and predictive models) Ongoing studies (continuous monitoring of batches in the market) Thanks to our high storage capacity and the use of chambers with environmental monitoring and real-time telemetry, we ensure traceability, reliability and stability of each sample throughout the entire process. This allows us to rigorously comply with international guidelines such as Codex Alimentarius, FDA, ICH, ISO 16779, ASTM and other specific regulations by product category. In addition, Aoxlab differentiates itself by offering a comprehensive and robust approach, by complementing stability studies with its own accredited analytical capabilities, which allows us to perform internally all the tests required during the study, such as: Physicochemical analysis Microbiological analysis Nutritional profiles Contaminants (heavy metals, pesticides, residual solvents) Cannabinoids and terpenes Allergens, moisture, color, pH, among many others We design each study based on product requirements, its matrix, packaging, storage conditions and current regulations. We apply statistical analysis tools and mathematical models such as the Arrhenius equation, regression curves and specification criteria to estimate and validate shelf life in a scientific and reliable manner.

Biodegradability

At Aoxlab we perform specialized analyses of aerobic biodegradability under controlled conditions, oriented to evaluate the environmental behavior of materials, products.


Products and packaging that seek to position themselves as sustainable alternatives to the use of conventional plastics. We have state-of-the-art equipment that allows us to perform studies under international standards ASTM D6400, ASTM D5338, designed to simulate biodegradation conditions in industrial composting with real-time monitoring. This methodology allows quantifying the degree of biodegradation of a product by measuring the CO₂ released as a result of microbial metabolism, continuously, accurately and reproducibly. Our services are directed to companies that develop or use biodegradable materials such as: Compostable packaging and containers Bioplastics and plastic substitutes Transformed plant material Ecological products with environmental certification Biodegradable raw materials Supplements, agricultural products and cosmetics with biodegradable components
